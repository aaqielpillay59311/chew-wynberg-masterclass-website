# Chew — Best-Principles Blueprint (Competitive Intelligence, Step 1)

Data-driven teardown of the best café / coffee-shop websites, translated into a build spec for **Chew** — a women-owned coffee shop, café & bakery at 76 Church Street, Wynberg, Cape Town (Mon–Fri 7am–4pm, 4.3★ Google, on Uber Eats, IG @chew_wynberg). Target: premium, editorial, mobile-first single-page marketing site.

**Method:** Firecrawl search across "best coffee shop website 2025", "award-winning cafe", "specialty roaster", "Cape Town coffee". Shortlisted 8, scored 5, deep-scraped 5 (markdown + branding tokens). Every recommendation below is grounded in what was actually observed on these live sites and cited inline.

## Winning Section Order (top→bottom for Chew)

Synthesised from the common skeleton across all five sites. One-line rationale each.

1. **Sticky nav** — logo left; anchor links (Menu · Our Story · Visit); persistent primary button "Order on Uber Eats". *Every strong site keeps an order/reserve CTA pinned; Haven pins "Reserve", Roots pins "Online Order And Pickup".*
2. **Announcement / open-now bar** (thin, dismissible) — "Open today 7am–4pm" or a seasonal line. *Lula runs a live "WE WILL BE CLOSED 7/3–7/4" notice; Haven runs seasonal "Ce printemps chez Haven" — timely = trust.*
3. **Hero** — full-bleed photo, H1 stating **what + where** ("Wynberg's neighbourhood coffee, breakfast & bakery"), warm one-line subcopy, primary CTA (Uber Eats) + secondary (Get directions), plus an open-now/hours chip. *Haven's H1 "Australian Brunch & Coffee Shop in the heart of Annecy" is the gold standard: category + geography in one line.*
4. **Signature / seasonal highlight** — 1–2 hero items (a signature bake, the flat white) with photo + short story + CTA. *Haven's "Spring Brunch" and "Guest Coffee: Black Mass" blocks make the page feel alive and give a reason to return.*
5. **Menu preview** — tabbed (Coffee · Breakfast · Bakery), each item = photo + one-line description + **price**; "See full menu" link. *Roots shows Roots Burrito $13.50 etc. with photo+desc+price right on the home — the single most useful conversion block.*
6. **The coffee / the craft** — beans, brewing methods, alternatives (chai, matcha, tea). *Haven: "espresso, cappuccino, Aussie-style flat white, filter…"; Truth lists provenance (region, variety, farmer). Signals specialty, not generic.*
7. **Our Story — women-owned & neighbourhood** — the human differentiator. *Roots' "Rooted in the Neighborhood" and Haven's "a warm place, a passionate team" prove the About block converts on warmth; Chew's women-owned angle is a unique hook none of the competitors have.*
8. **Gallery** — art-directed food + space photography (grid or carousel). *All five lean on professional imagery; Lula even credits "All Photos by @kylekissel".*
9. **Reviews / social proof** — testimonial carousel (name + date), the 4.3★ Google rating, any press. *Truth's 9-quote wall with named guests + "Daily Telegraph" press is best-in-class and directly liftable.*
10. **Visit Us — location & hours** — embedded Google map, full NAP, weekly hours table, "Get directions", Uber Eats, IG link. *Roots and Lula both nail this: map + address (tel:/mailto: links) + day-by-day hours + directions.*
11. **Footer** — NAP repeat, hours, IG @chew_wynberg, order link, back-to-top. *Reinforces the two things a local searcher needs: how to get there and how to order.*

## Above-the-Fold Rules (what the best heroes do)

- **State category + location in the H1.** Haven: "Australian Brunch & Coffee Shop **in the heart of Annecy**". For Chew: name the trio (coffee / breakfast / bakery) + "Wynberg".
- **Emotional one-liner under the H1, not a feature list.** Haven: "We believe coffee and food are more than simple pleasures: they're moments to connect… Welcome to Haven!" Warmth over specs.
- **One dominant, high-quality hero image** (food or interior), full-bleed. Every winner leads with a photograph, not a logo-on-color.
- **Two CTAs, clear hierarchy** — one filled primary (order), one outline secondary (directions/menu). Haven = Reserve (filled terracotta) + Gifting (outline).
- **Surface trust instantly.** Truth puts its award line *above the fold* ("named 'The World's Best Coffee Shop'"). Chew's equivalent: "4.3★ on Google · Loved in Wynberg since [year]".
- **Show an open-now / hours signal** near the fold so a hungry local knows they can come now.

---

## CTA / Offer Rules

- **Pin ONE primary action everywhere.** For Chew that's **"Order on Uber Eats"** (its real transaction path), mirrored in nav, hero, and menu section — Roots pins "Online Order And Pickup" top and bottom.
- **Secondary = low-commitment:** "Get directions", "See the menu", "View hours". Haven pairs Reserve with a softer Gifting; the primary is always the money action.
- **Verb-first, concrete button copy** observed: "Order online", "Make a Reservation", "Explore our menu", "Get directions", "Brunch time", "Coffee time". Avoid vague "Learn more".
- **Risk-reversal for a café = friction removal, not guarantees:** show price, show hours, show the map, one-tap `tel:` and `mailto:` links (Roots uses both), one-tap directions. Reducing "will they be open / where is it / what's it cost" is the conversion lever.
- **Give a reason to act now:** a rotating seasonal special (Haven) or a signature item callout creates urgency without discounting a premium brand.

---

## Copy Principles (voice & headline patterns)

- **Voice = warm, first-person-plural, hospitable.** "We believe…", "Welcome to Haven!", "Pull up a chair — you're part of it now" (Roots). Chew should sound like a host, not a brochure.
- **Transformation over transaction.** Best sites sell the *moment*, not the SKU: Haven "moments to connect, to travel differently, to feel right at home"; Truth "Experience the Joy of the Extraordinary". Chew: sell the ritual (morning-in-Wynberg feeling), then the flat white.
- **Headline patterns observed:**
  - Category + place: "Australian Brunch & Coffee Shop in the heart of Annecy" (Haven).
  - Benefit + warmth: "Explore specialty coffee in a warm, welcoming atmosphere" (Haven).
  - Community: "Rooted in the Neighborhood" (Roots).
  - Provenance flex: "The World's Most Luxurious Coffee" (Truth).
- **Editorial / cultural borrow.** Lula quotes Patti Smith's *M Train* listing famous cafés (incl. Lula) — literary credibility. Chew can channel this with a short editorial line about Church Street / Wynberg.
- **Name your suppliers & craft** to prove quality. Truth names its egg farm, beef supplier, and bakery with links. Chew: name the roaster, the baker, the local sourcing.
- **Lead with the differentiator:** none of the five are women-owned — Chew should say it plainly and proudly in Story + meta description.

## Menu Presentation Patterns

- **Best pattern (steal this): featured items on the homepage with photo + one-line description + price.** Roots: "Roots Burrito — Egg, cheddar, avocado, tomato & black bean spread in a wrap. $13.50" with a thumbnail. This is the highest-utility block observed.
- **Tab / category the menu** — Coffee, Breakfast, Bakery — so mobile users scan fast.
- **Seasonal rotation** keeps it fresh: Haven swaps a "Spring Brunch" and monthly "Guest Coffee" — signals a living kitchen and gives return-visit reasons.
- **Tasting-note / provenance style for coffee** (Truth): altitude, process, variety, region, farmer. A lighter version ("notes of blackberry, nougat") lifts perceived quality.
- **Always link to a full menu**; the homepage shows a curated 4–8 items, not the whole list (Roots "Explore our menu", Haven "The Aussie brunch").
- **Photography per item is non-negotiable** at the premium tier — every food item on Roots/Haven has its own shot.

---

## Social Proof / Reviews Patterns

- **Testimonial carousel with attribution** is the workhorse. Truth runs **9 named reviews with dates** ("— Rachel M. | 8 March 2020") plus press pull-quotes ("Truth Coffee is ultimately the world's best coffee shop — Daily Telegraph"). Directly liftable for Chew.
- **Lead with a headline stat / accolade.** Truth's award and Haven's "France's best coffee shop 2024" do heavy lifting. Chew's equivalent: **"4.3★ on Google"** + a review count, shown as a badge.
- **Press / third-party credibility beats self-praise.** Lula borrows Patti Smith + Michelin/James Beard; Truth borrows The Telegraph. Chew: quote a local reviewer, an Uber Eats rating, or an IG feature.
- **Real names + real detail** ("Loved the barista, Josh") read as authentic; avoid anonymous "Great coffee!!!".
- **Place proof twice:** a compact rating near the hero, and the full carousel lower down before "Visit Us".

## Local Business / SEO Patterns (a Wynberg café must-do)

- **NAP everywhere, machine-readable.** Roots shows name + "639 5th Ave, Brooklyn" + `tel:` + `mailto:` + "Get directions". Lula wires the address straight to a Google Maps place URL and the phone to `tel:`. Chew: 76 Church Street, Wynberg, Cape Town — as clickable links.
- **Embed a live Google Map** (Roots does) so directions are one tap.
- **Explicit weekly hours table** (Roots lists every day; Lula shows "WEDNESDAY–MONDAY, CLOSED TUESDAYS" + holiday notice). Chew: Mon–Fri 7am–4pm, and state weekend/closed days clearly.
- **Add JSON-LD schema** (none of the competitors visibly nailed this — a chance to out-optimize):
  - `CafeOrCoffeeShop` / `Restaurant` (subtype of `LocalBusiness`) with `name`, `address` (PostalAddress), `geo`, `telephone`, `openingHoursSpecification`, `servesCuisine`, `priceRange`, `image`, `url`, `sameAs` (IG, Uber Eats).
  - `AggregateRating` (4.3★) + `Review` objects for the testimonials.
  - `Menu` / `hasMenu` linking the menu section.
  - `BreadcrumbList` and `Organization` for the brand.
- **Keywords a Wynberg café should target** (from what these sites rank on — Lula's meta is a clean model; Truth's is a *bad* stuffed model): "coffee shop Wynberg", "café Wynberg Cape Town", "breakfast/brunch Wynberg", "bakery Wynberg", "specialty coffee Cape Town Southern Suburbs", "best coffee Church Street Wynberg", "women-owned café Cape Town", "Wynberg flat white / pastries". Keep it in the meta description + H1 + Visit section naturally.
- **hreflang / language**: not needed (single EN/local audience) — Haven runs EN/FR because it serves tourists; Chew stays EN-ZA. Set `lang="en-ZA"`.
- **Fast, crawlable, single-page**: static/hand-built beats Truth's WordPress+Elementor+Slider Revolution+WPBakery stack (heavy, slow, artifact-prone).

## Visual Design Patterns

- **Palette = warm, coffee-derived, light mode.** Every scraped site uses light `colorScheme` with an espresso-brown + cream + warm accent:
  - Haven: primary `#2B1A12` espresso, secondary `#EFDACC` cream, accent `#C1643B` terracotta, bg `#FFFAF7` warm-white.
  - Roots: primary/accent `#8C5749` mocha on `#FFFFFF`.
  - Common Grounds: accent `#F9925F` orange, secondary `#FFE6CC` peach, text `#313322` olive-black.
  - **Chew direction:** warm off-white background + deep espresso text + one terracotta/burnt-orange or warm-brown accent for CTAs. (Avoid cold greys/blues.)
- **Type = characterful display + clean sans body.** Haven pairs a script display ("With Hearty") + bold-italic DIN heading + DIN body. Roots pairs a serif heading (Bitter) / display (Eveleth) + humanist sans body (Rubik). **Editorial move for Chew:** a distinctive serif or script for headlines, a clean neutral sans for body.
- **Button shape is a brand signal:** Haven = fully rounded pills (30px radius, filled terracotta primary + outline secondary); Roots = hard square (0px). Pick one and be consistent — pills read friendlier/premium-editorial.
- **Generous spacing, small base unit.** Haven base unit 12, 5px radius; roomy sections. Premium = whitespace, not density.
- **Hand-drawn / line-art accents** add craft warmth — Haven uses SVG line-art of its storefront and hand-lettered section labels ("BRUNCH", "COFFEE"). A cheap, high-impact editorial device.
- **Thin dividers / section labels** and section eyebrow labels (Haven's small caps "Ce printemps chez Haven") structure the long scroll.
- **Photography is the hero medium** — large, warm, art-directed food + interior shots carry the design on every winner.

## Interactive Elements Worth Stealing (UI-sniping candidates)

- **Sticky nav that keeps the order CTA visible** on scroll (Haven "Reserve", Roots "Online Order And Pickup").
- **Seasonal/rotating feature blocks** — Haven's "this spring / guest coffee this month" pattern; makes a static single-page feel alive.
- **Dish image carousel** — Haven loops a row of 8 square dish photos; auto-advancing, swipeable on mobile.
- **Testimonial slider with dots/arrows** — Truth's 9-item numbered carousel; low effort, high trust.
- **Embedded interactive Google Map** with a custom pin (Roots).
- **Scroll-reveal / fade-up on sections** — expected at the Awwwards tier (Haven). Keep subtle and performant.
- **Tabbed menu** (Coffee / Breakfast / Bakery) for fast mobile scanning.
- **Open-now chip / live hours** computed from the schedule.
- **One-tap actions**: `tel:`, `mailto:`, "Get directions", Uber Eats deep link (Roots/Lula).
- **Announcement bar** for seasonal or holiday notices (Lula, Haven).
- *(Skip the 3D/heavy experiments like Caffè Design's WebGL — overkill and slow for a local café; Chew wins on warmth + speed.)*

---

## What to AVOID (mistakes seen on weaker café sites)

- **Keyword-stuffed meta** — Truth's `<meta keywords>` crams ~60 terms ("hipster, steampunk, DDT, robusta…"). Google ignores it and it reads spammy. Use one honest, local, benefit-led description.
- **CMS artifacts leaking to the page** — Truth renders a raw `[qode_pricing_list]` shortcode on the live homepage. Ship clean; hand-build or QA the template.
- **Plugin/page-builder bloat** — Truth stacks WordPress + Elementor + WPBakery + Slider Revolution → slow, heavy, fragile. Keep Chew lean and fast (mobile-first means performance-first).
- **Disabling zoom** — Truth sets `user-scalable=no`; an accessibility and mobile-UX fail. Never block pinch-zoom.
- **No prices / no real menu on the page** — Common Grounds routes everything to an external ordering app and shows no menu or dish info; visitors bounce. Show curated items + prices (Roots pattern).
- **No reviews / no social proof** — Roots and Common Grounds show none on home; they leave trust on the table. Chew has a 4.3★ asset — use it.
- **Burying location & hours** — if a local can't instantly find address, hours, and directions, the site fails its core job. Make "Visit Us" a first-class section, not a footer afterthought.
- **Logo-on-color hero with no photograph** — weak cafés open cold; winners open with an appetite-triggering image.
- **Generic stock photography / inconsistent shots** — premium tier uses cohesive, art-directed, credited photography (Lula, Haven). Mismatched phone snaps cheapen the brand.
- **Cold/corporate palette or vague copy** ("Learn more", "Welcome to our website") — kills the warmth that sells a café.
